00 — The throughline

Build the machine that builds the thing.

A designer and illustrator who became an engineer, then a founder — and settled on the work he loves most: making products real, and building the systems that produce them.

Where it starts Designer & illustrator Form, composition, the feel of a thing before it works. Then became an engineer Architecture, data, services — the structure that makes it real. Now and a founder The whole system — prototype to real users, running on its own.

Across two decades the throughline is consistent: build the machine that builds the thing, then engineer it to run without standing over it. He has done this in both software and physical manufacturing — a rare pairing, and the core of what he builds now.

His range spans front-end and backend architecture, commerce and payments infrastructure, applied AI, design systems, and end-to-end production operations. He can define a product thesis, decompose a system into clean service boundaries, design the interaction model, and carry the result through to launch, fulfillment, and iteration.

He knows the difference between a demo, a product, a platform, and a business — and has shipped all four. That makes him effective where a company needs more than an idea: a product architecture, a technical narrative, a commercial path, and a system that survives real users.
